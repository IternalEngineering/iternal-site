AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) are about being the business that AI engines recommend, cite and describe accurately when someone asks them a question.

In plain English

Two acronyms, one job.

AEO is about answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google's AI Overviews — making sure they find you and represent you correctly when a customer asks a question in your space.

GEO is the broader practice of optimising for generative AI as a discovery channel, including how AI models are trained on and cite your content over time.

In practice they overlap. We treat them as one service because the work is the same: make your business legible to AI, build the authority signals AI trusts, and check what AI is actually saying about you.

Why this matters now.

If your customers are starting to ask AI instead of Google — and they are — then "show up in AI answers" is the same problem "show up on Google" was fifteen years ago. The window to get ahead of it is now, while most of your competitors haven't noticed.

Common questions.

Is AEO / GEO just SEO with a new name?
No. SEO optimises for ranking in a list of links. AEO optimises for being the answer AI gives. The work overlaps (good content, clean structure, authority) but the goal, the metrics and a lot of the tactics are genuinely different. SEO experts who haven't retooled won't get you there.
How do you measure whether it's working?
We test the actual questions your customers ask, against the actual AI engines they use, every month. You see your visibility score climb (or not) in plain numbers. No dashboards full of vanity metrics. If it's not working, we'll tell you.
How long until we see results?
The audit takes 1–2 weeks. Quick wins from the optimise stage often show up within 30 days. Meaningful, defensible improvement in AI visibility usually takes 60–120 days, depending on how competitive your space is and how well-known you currently are.
Do we need to be doing AEO and SEO?
Yes — for now. Most of your customers still use Google for some queries. Our work strengthens both at once because the underlying content and authority improvements are good for both kinds of search. You don't have to choose.
